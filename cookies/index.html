Cookies Policy

How we use cookies and similar technologies on our website.

Last updated: February 2026

This Cookies Policy explains what cookies are, how Strength 9 Ltd ("we", "us", or "our") uses them on our website at strength9.co.uk, and your choices regarding their use.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to website owners.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use a minimal number of cookies to ensure our website functions properly and to understand how visitors interact with our content. We do not use cookies for advertising or to track you across other websites.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They are usually set in response to actions you take, such as setting your privacy preferences or filling in forms.

Cookie Purpose Duration
cookie_consent Stores your cookie consent preference 1 year

3.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors use our website by collecting anonymous, aggregated data. This information helps us improve our website's performance and content.

Cookie Purpose Duration
_ga Google Analytics — distinguishes unique visitors 2 years
_ga_* Google Analytics — maintains session state 2 years

3.3 Third-Party Cookies

Our contact form is processed by Formspree. When you submit a form, Formspree may set cookies to prevent spam and ensure the form works correctly. These cookies are governed by Formspree's privacy policy.

4. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

  • Browser settings: Most web browsers allow you to manage cookies through their settings. You can typically find these in the "Options", "Settings", or "Preferences" menu of your browser.
  • Deleting cookies: You can delete cookies already stored on your device through your browser settings.
  • Blocking cookies: You can set your browser to block all cookies or only third-party cookies. Note that blocking all cookies may affect the functionality of some websites.

For more information on managing cookies in specific browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge
